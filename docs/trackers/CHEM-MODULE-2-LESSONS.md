## Overview

Module 2 focuses on the "Chef's Math" of Chemistry — measuring and calculating. Students learn to quantify chemical reactions using the mole concept, perform stoichiometric calculations, and work with gases and solutions.

## Syllabus Alignment Notes

### HSC Chemistry Module 2 Topics Covered:

- **Inquiry Question 1:** How are measurements made in chemistry?
  - Chemical equations and balancing
  - Law of conservation of mass
  - Significant figures and precision
  - Dimensional analysis

- **Inquiry Question 2:** How are amounts of substances measured in chemistry?
  - The mole concept
  - Molar mass
  - Empirical and molecular formulas

- **Inquiry Question 3:** How does the quantity of materials influence the outcome of reactions?
  - Stoichiometry (mole-to-mole, mass-to-mass)
  - Limiting reagents
  - Yield calculations

- **Inquiry Question 4:** How are solutions prepared and measured?
  - Concentration units (molarity, g/L, ppm)
  - Molarity calculations
  - Dilutions
  - Standard solutions

- **Inquiry Question 5:** How do gases behave and how can we measure them?
  - Gas laws (Boyle's, Charles's, Gay-Lussac's, Avogadro's)
  - Combined and ideal gas laws
  - Molar volume
  - Gas stoichiometry
