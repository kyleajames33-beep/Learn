## Overview

Module 2 scales up from individual cells to how cells team up to form tissues, organs, and complex systems in both plants and animals. Students will explore the hierarchy of organisation, nutrient and gas exchange systems in plants and animals, and transport systems.

## Syllabus Alignment Notes

### HSC Biology Module 2 Topics Covered:
- **Inquiry Question 1:** How are cells arranged in a multicellular organism?
  - Cell differentiation and specialisation
  - Hierarchy of organisation
  - Animal and plant tissues

- **Inquiry Question 2:** How do plants transport water and nutrients?
  - Leaf structure and photosynthesis
  - Gas exchange in plants
  - Root systems and water uptake
  - Xylem and phloem transport

- **Inquiry Question 3:** How does the composition of the transport medium change as it moves around an organism?
  - Digestive system structure and function
  - Gas exchange systems (mammals, fish, insects)
  - Circulatory systems (open vs closed)
  - Blood composition and blood vessels
  - Heart structure and function
  - Changes in blood composition

## Dependencies from Module 1

Before starting Module 2, students should know:
- Cell structure (prokaryotic vs eukaryotic)
- Cell organelles and their functions
- Membrane structure (fluid mosaic model)
- Transport mechanisms (diffusion, osmosis, active transport)
- Enzyme structure and function
