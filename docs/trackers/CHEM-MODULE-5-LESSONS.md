## Overview

Module 5 explores the "Tug-of-War" in reversible reactions. Students investigate dynamic equilibrium, factors affecting equilibrium position, solubility equilibria, and the mathematical treatment of equilibrium constants.

## Syllabus Alignment Notes

### HSC Chemistry Module 5 Topics Covered:

- **Inquiry Question 1:** What happens when chemical reactions reach equilibrium?
  - Static vs dynamic equilibrium
  - Reversible reactions
  - Open vs closed systems
  - Models of equilibrium

- **Inquiry Question 2:** How can Le Chatelier's principle be used to predict the effect of changes in conditions on a system at equilibrium?
  - Le Chatelier's Principle
  - Concentration, pressure, and temperature effects
  - Practical investigations

- **Inquiry Question 3:** How can the equilibrium constant be used to predict the position of equilibrium?
  - Equilibrium constant (Keq)
  - Magnitude of K
  - ICE tables
  - Reaction quotient (Q)

- **Inquiry Question 4:** What are the solubility products and how can they be used?
  - Solution equilibria
  - Solubility product (Ksp)
  - Common ion effect
  - Predicting precipitation

- **Inquiry Question 5:** How does solubility relate to chemical equilibrium?
  - Calculating Ksp
  - Thermodynamics and equilibrium
  - Entropy and equilibrium position

## Key Equations Summary

| Equation/Expression | Usage |
|---------------------|-------|
| Keq = [products]/[reactants] | Equilibrium constant |
| Q = [products]initial/[reactants]initial | Reaction quotient |
| Ksp = [ions]products | Solubility product |
| ΔG° = -RT ln(K) | Thermodynamics link |
