## Overview

Module 6 explores mutations, biotechnology, and the future of DNA. Students investigate how genetic changes occur, their impacts on organisms, and how human technology is harnessing genetic knowledge for agriculture, medicine, and industry.

## Syllabus Alignment Notes

### HSC Biology Module 6 Topics Covered:

- **Inquiry Question 1:** How does mutation introduce new alleles into a population?
  - Mutagens and their effects
  - Types of mutations (point and chromosomal)
  - Somatic vs germ-line mutations
  - Coding vs non-coding mutations
  - Sources of genetic variation
  - Gene flow and genetic drift

- **Inquiry Question 2:** What is the role of genetic technologies in society and agriculture?
  - Biotechnology history (past, present, future)
  - Agricultural applications (Bt cotton, Golden Rice)
  - Industrial applications (biofuels, bioremediation)
  - Impact on biodiversity

- **Inquiry Question 3:** What is the role of genetic technologies in medicine?
  - Medical applications (insulin, vaccines)
  - Gene therapy
  - Genomics and the Human Genome Project
  - Bioinformatics

- **Inquiry Question 4:** What is the effect of new reproductive technologies on genetic variation in populations?
  - Cloning (whole organism and gene)
  - Recombinant DNA technology
  - Artificial insemination and pollination revisited
  - Transgenic species

- **Inquiry Question 5:** What ethical issues arise from the use of biotechnology?
  - Social and ethical implications
  - Regulation of biotechnology
  - Statistical analysis and efficacy
  - Innovation vs ethics balance
