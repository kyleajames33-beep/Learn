## Overview

Module 4 explores the "Why" behind chemical reactions — enthalpy, entropy, and spontaneity. Students learn to predict whether reactions will occur and understand the energy changes involved.

## Syllabus Alignment Notes

### HSC Chemistry Module 4 Topics Covered:

- **Inquiry Question 1:** What energy changes occur in chemical reactions?
  - System vs surroundings
  - Enthalpy (ΔH)
  - Exothermic vs endothermic
  - Energy profile diagrams
  - Catalysts and activation energy

- **Inquiry Question 2:** How is energy measured in chemical reactions?
  - Calorimetry (q = mcΔT)
  - Molar heat of combustion and dissolution
  - Enthalpy of neutralization
  - Thermochemical equations

- **Inquiry Question 3:** How do we calculate energy changes?
  - Hess's Law
  - Bond enthalpies
  - Enthalpy of formation

- **Inquiry Question 4:** What role does entropy play in chemical reactions?
  - Entropy (S) and disorder
  - Entropy changes in physical and chemical processes
  - Entropy in phase changes

- **Inquiry Question 5:** How can we predict if a reaction will occur?
  - Spontaneity
  - Gibbs Free Energy (ΔG = ΔH - TΔS)
  - Temperature dependence of spontaneity
  - Photosynthesis and respiration comparison

## Key Equations Summary

| Equation | Usage |
|----------|-------|
| q = mcΔT | Heat transfer calculations |
| ΔH = -q/n | Molar enthalpy |
| ΔG = ΔH - TΔS | Gibbs free energy |
| ΔH° = ΣΔHf°(products) - ΣΔHf°(reactants) | Hess's law with formation |
| ΔH = ΣBE(bonds broken) - ΣBE(bonds formed) | Bond enthalpy method |
