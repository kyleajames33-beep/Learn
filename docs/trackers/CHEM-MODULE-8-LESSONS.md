## Overview

Module 8 focuses on the "Detective Work" of chemistry — identifying and measuring substances. Students master qualitative and quantitative analysis techniques, spectroscopy, and chemical synthesis while considering environmental and social impacts.

## Syllabus Alignment Notes

### HSC Chemistry Module 8 Topics Covered:

- **Inquiry Question 1:** How are qualitative and quantitative analyses performed in chemistry?
  - Analysis of inorganic ions
  - Flame tests and precipitation
  - Anion identification
  - Complexation reactions
  - Gravimetric analysis
  - Colorimetry

- **Inquiry Question 2:** How are spectroscopic techniques used to determine the structure and concentration of substances?
  - UV-Visible spectroscopy
  - Atomic absorption spectroscopy
  - Infrared spectroscopy
  - Mass spectrometry
  - Proton NMR spectroscopy
  - Carbon-13 NMR spectroscopy
  - Integration of techniques

- **Inquiry Question 3:** How is information about the reactivity and structure of organic compounds obtained?
  - Testing for organic functional groups
  - Spectroscopic analysis of organic compounds

- **Inquiry Question 4:** How can chemical substances be synthesized and measured?
  - Chemical synthesis (reagents, conditions, yield, purity, atom economy)
  - Environmental and social impacts
  - Industrial processes

- **Inquiry Question 5:** How can chemistry be used to monitor the environment?
  - Environmental monitoring (nitrogen oxides, ozone)
  - Water quality analysis
  - Role of analytical chemistry
