## Overview

Module 8 explores non-infectious diseases and disorders, covering homeostasis, genetic and environmental diseases, epidemiology, sensory systems, and medical technologies. This is the final module of the HSC Biology course.

## Syllabus Alignment Notes

### HSC Biology Module 8 Topics Covered:

- **Inquiry Question 1:** How is an organism's internal environment maintained in response to a changing external environment?
  - Homeostasis and feedback mechanisms
  - Temperature regulation (endotherms vs ectotherms)
  - Water balance (ADH, kidneys)
  - Blood glucose regulation (insulin, glucagon)
  - Neural pathways (CNS vs PNS)

- **Inquiry Question 2:** What are the causes, processes, and effects of non-infectious diseases?
  - Genetic diseases (single gene and chromosomal)
  - Environmental diseases
  - Nutritional diseases
  - Cancer biology

- **Inquiry Question 3:** How are diseases detected, prevented, and treated?
  - Epidemiology (descriptive, analytical, interventional)
  - Treatment vs management

- **Inquiry Question 4:** How are technologies used to assist people who have disorders?
  - The ear (structure, hearing process, hearing loss, technology)
  - The eye (structure, vision process, disorders, technology)
  - The kidney (structure, malfunction, dialysis)
  - Transplants and future technologies
  - Evaluating technology benefits and limitations
