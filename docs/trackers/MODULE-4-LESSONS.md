## Overview

Module 4 examines ecosystem dynamics through time — from the past (paleontological evidence), through the present (population interactions and human impacts), to the future (climate change and conservation). Students learn to analyze and predict ecosystem changes.

## Syllabus Alignment Notes

### HSC Biology Module 4 Topics Covered:

- **Inquiry Question 1:** How do selection pressures within an ecosystem influence evolutionary change?
  - Population dynamics and sampling
  - Biotic relationships (predation, competition, symbiosis)
  - Ecosystem structure (energy flow, niches)

- **Inquiry Question 2:** How do the Earth's processes affect living organisms and their environments?
  - Environmental changes (short and long term)
  - Paleontological evidence (rock art, ice cores)
  - Evolution of Australian biota

- **Inquiry Question 3:** How can human activity affect the continuation of species?
  - Human impacts (land clearing, over-exploitation, introduced species, pollution)
  - Climate change
  - Conservation and restoration
  - Future modeling and technological solutions
