## Overview

Module 7 focuses on the chemistry of carbon — the foundation of life. Students explore hydrocarbons, functional groups, organic reactions, polymers, and the synthesis of organic compounds.

## Syllabus Alignment Notes

### HSC Chemistry Module 7 Topics Covered:

- **Inquiry Question 1:** How can hydrocarbons be classified and represented?
  - Carbon bonding and tetravalence
  - Alkanes, alkenes, alkynes
  - Isomerism
  - IUPAC nomenclature

- **Inquiry Question 2:** How do functional groups affect the physical and chemical properties of organic compounds?
  - Alcohols
  - Haloalkanes
  - Aldehydes and ketones
  - Carboxylic acids and esters
  - Amines and amides
  - Intermolecular forces
  - Solubility

- **Inquiry Question 3:** How can organic compounds be arranged into reaction pathways?
  - Reactions of alkanes, alkenes, and alcohols
  - Biofuels
  - Esterification
  - Organic acids and bases
  - Soaps and detergents

- **Inquiry Question 4:** How can polymers be designed for a purpose?
  - Addition polymerization
  - Condensation polymerization
  - Polymer properties and applications

- **Inquiry Question 5:** How are organic compounds processed and synthesized safely?
  - Safe handling procedures
  - Reaction pathways and synthesis planning
  - Practical synthesis techniques
