**Module:** Biological Diversity

## Overview

Module 3 explores how life changes over time and how we categorize it. Students investigate the mechanisms of evolution, the evidence supporting it, and the resulting diversity of life on Earth.

## Syllabus Alignment Notes

### HSC Biology Module 3 Topics Covered:

- **Inquiry Question 1:** How do environmental pressures promote a change in species diversity and abundance?
  - Selection pressures (abiotic and biotic)
  - Adaptations (structural, physiological, behavioural)
  - Natural selection

- **Inquiry Question 2:** How do adaptations increase the organism's ability to survive?
  - Types of adaptations
  - Case studies of Australian species
  - Form-function relationships

- **Inquiry Question 3:** What is the relationship between evolution and biodiversity?
  - Speciation mechanisms
  - Evolutionary patterns (convergent, divergent)
  - Evidence for evolution (biochemical, anatomical, embryological, biogeographical, fossil)
  - Dating techniques
  - Phylogenetic trees
