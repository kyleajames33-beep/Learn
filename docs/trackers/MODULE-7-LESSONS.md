## Overview

Module 7 explores pathogens, the immune response, and global health. Students investigate different types of disease-causing organisms, how they spread, how the body defends itself, and strategies for controlling infectious diseases at individual and population levels.

## Syllabus Alignment Notes

### HSC Biology Module 7 Topics Covered:

- **Inquiry Question 1:** What causes infectious disease in plants and animals?
  - Types of pathogens (bacteria, viruses, fungi, protozoa, prions, parasites)
  - Modes of transmission
  - Koch's postulates and germ theory
  - Plant and animal diseases

- **Inquiry Question 2:** How does the body respond to infectious disease?
  - First line of defence (physical and chemical barriers)
  - Second line of defence (innate immunity: inflammation, phagocytosis)
  - Lymphatic system
  - Third line of defence (adaptive immunity: B-cells, T-cells)
  - Primary and secondary immune responses

- **Inquiry Question 3:** How can the spread of infectious disease be limited?
  - Quarantine and isolation
  - Public health campaigns
  - Pesticides and vector control
  - Antibiotics and antivirals
  - Vaccination (active vs passive)
  - Herd immunity
  - Environmental management

- **Inquiry Question 4:** What approaches can be taken to manage infectious disease?
  - Traditional medicine approaches
  - Epidemiology and disease tracking
  - Case studies of disease management
