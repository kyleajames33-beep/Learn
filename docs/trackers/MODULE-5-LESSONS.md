## Overview

Module 5 marks the beginning of the HSC course (Year 12). It explores how traits are passed from one generation to the next, covering reproduction mechanisms, cell division, DNA structure and function, protein synthesis, and inheritance patterns.

## Syllabus Alignment Notes

### HSC Biology Module 5 Topics Covered:

- **Inquiry Question 1:** How does reproduction ensure the continuity of a species?
  - Reproduction mechanisms (animals, plants, microorganisms)
  - Fertilization and hormonal control
  - Artificial reproductive technologies

- **Inquiry Question 2:** How important is it for genetic material to be replicated exactly?
  - Mitosis and meiosis
  - DNA structure and replication
  - Prokaryotic vs eukaryotic DNA

- **Inquiry Question 3:** How are characteristics of an organism inherited?
  - Polypeptide synthesis (transcription and translation)
  - Protein structure and function
  - Mutations and variation
  - Gene-environment interactions

- **Inquiry Question 4:** How is the phenotype of an organism determined?
  - Mendelian and non-Mendelian genetics
  - Sex-linkage
  - Pedigree analysis
  - Population genetics

- **Inquiry Question 5:** How are species genetically different from each other?
  - DNA sequencing and profiling
  - SNP analysis
