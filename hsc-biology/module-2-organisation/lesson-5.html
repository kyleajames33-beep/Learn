Cell Differentiation & Specialisation

How does a single fertilised egg become a complex organism with hundreds of different cell types? Discover the remarkable process of cell differentiation and how cells become specialised for specific functions.

Learning Objectives

Explain how unspecialised cells develop into specialised cell types

Tissue Formation

Understand how specialised cells form tissues

What is Cell Differentiation?

Cell differentiation is the process by which a cell becomes specialised in structure and function. It's how a single zygote (fertilised egg) develops into a complex multicellular organism with over 200 different cell types.

Key Principle: All cells in an organism contain the same DNA (genome), but different genes are expressed (activated) in different cells, leading to different structures and functions.

HSC Connection

Key Exam Points

  • Explain the relationship between structure and function in specialised cells
  • Compare the adaptations of cells in different tissues
  • Describe how gene expression leads to cell specialisation
  • Predict the consequences of abnormal cell differentiation
Exam Tip: When describing cell adaptations, always link the structural feature directly to its function. For example: "Red blood cells have a biconcave shape which increases surface area for efficient oxygen diffusion."
