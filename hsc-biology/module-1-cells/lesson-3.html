Cell City Analogy

A cell is like a tiny city! The nucleus is the town hall (control center), mitochondria are power plants, ribosomes are factories, and the cell membrane is the city border. Every organelle has a specific job that keeps the cell functioning.

What are Eukaryotic Cells?

Eukaryotic cells are cells that contain a membrane-bound nucleus and other membrane-bound organelles. The term comes from Greek meaning "true kernel" (nucleus).

Eukaryotic Cell: A cell that contains a true nucleus enclosed by a nuclear membrane, along with various membrane-bound organelles. Includes all cells of animals, plants, fungi, and protists.

Eukaryotic cells are typically 10-100 micrometres in diameter - much larger than prokaryotic cells. This larger size is possible because of compartmentalization: different organelles perform specialized functions.

The Nucleus: Control Center

The nucleus is the defining feature of eukaryotic cells. It houses the cell's genetic material (DNA) and controls all cellular activities.

Nuclear Envelope

Double membrane with nuclear pores that controls what enters and exits the nucleus.

Chromatin/DNA

Genetic material organized into chromosomes. Contains instructions for all cell functions.

Nucleolus

Dense region where ribosomal RNA (rRNA) is produced and ribosomes begin assembly.

Nuclear Pores

Protein complexes that regulate transport between nucleus and cytoplasm.

Key Organelles and Their Functions

Mitochondria

The "powerhouse" of the cell. Produces ATP (energy) through cellular respiration. Has its own DNA (evidence of endosymbiotic origin).

Key point: Muscle cells have thousands of mitochondria because they need lots of energy!

Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER)

A network of membranes throughout the cell:

  • Rough ER: Studded with ribosomes; synthesizes proteins
  • Smooth ER: No ribosomes; synthesizes lipids and detoxifies

Golgi Apparatus

The "post office" of the cell. Modifies, packages, and ships proteins and lipids to their final destinations. Made of flattened membrane sacs called cisternae.

Ribosomes

Protein synthesis factories. Found free in cytoplasm or attached to rough ER. Smaller than organelles (not membrane-bound).

Lysosomes

Contain digestive enzymes to break down waste, damaged organelles, and foreign invaders. The "recycling center" of the cell.

Deep Dive: Endosymbiotic Theory

The endosymbiotic theory proposes that some organelles in eukaryotic cells were once free-living prokaryotes that were engulfed by larger cells.

Evidence supporting this theory:

  • Mitochondria and chloroplasts have their own DNA (circular, like bacteria)
  • They have double membranes (one from engulfing, one original)
  • They replicate independently by binary fission
  • They have their own ribosomes (similar to bacterial ribosomes)

Animal vs Plant Cells

Feature Animal Cell Plant Cell
Cell Wall Absent Present (cellulose)
Chloroplasts Absent Present (photosynthesis)
Large Central Vacuole Absent (small vacuoles) Present
Lysosomes Present Rare/uncommon
Centrioles Present Absent (in most)
Key Plant Cell Structures:
  • Cell Wall: Provides structural support and protection (rigid, made of cellulose)
  • Chloroplasts: Site of photosynthesis; contain chlorophyll (green pigment)
  • Large Central Vacuole: Stores water, nutrients, and waste; maintains turgor pressure

Copy Into Your Books

Key Definitions:

  • Eukaryote: Organism with membrane-bound nucleus and organelles
  • Nucleus: Control center containing genetic material (DNA)
  • Mitochondria: Organelle producing ATP through cellular respiration
  • Chloroplast: Plant organelle where photosynthesis occurs
  • Endosymbiotic Theory: Theory that mitochondria and chloroplasts evolved from ancient bacteria

Assessment Questions

1. Which organelle is responsible for producing ATP?

2. Which structure is found in plant cells but NOT in animal cells?

3. What is the function of the nucleolus?

Short Answer

1. Describe two pieces of evidence supporting the endosymbiotic theory. (2 marks)

Answer Key

Multiple Choice:

  1. B - Mitochondria produce ATP through cellular respiration
  2. C - Chloroplasts are unique to plant cells (and some algae)
  3. A - The nucleolus produces rRNA and assembles ribosomes

Short Answer:

Any two of: Mitochondria/chloroplasts have their own circular DNA; they have double membranes; they replicate by binary fission; they have their own ribosomes similar to bacteria.
