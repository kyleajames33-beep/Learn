Prokaryotic Cells

Did You Know?

Bacteria are the most abundant organisms on Earth. There are more bacterial cells in your body than human cells! Don't worry though - most bacteria are harmless or even beneficial to our health.

What are Prokaryotic Cells?

Prokaryotic cells are the simplest type of cells. They lack a membrane-bound nucleus and other membrane-bound organelles. The term "prokaryote" comes from Greek meaning "before kernel" (nucleus).

Prokaryotic Cell: A cell that lacks a membrane-bound nucleus and membrane-bound organelles. DNA is found in a region called the nucleoid. Includes bacteria and archaea.

All prokaryotes are unicellular organisms. They are typically much smaller than eukaryotic cells (1-10 μm) and have a simpler internal structure.

Prokaryotic Cell Structure

Cell Wall

Provides structural support and protection. In bacteria, it contains peptidoglycan, a unique molecule not found in other organisms.

Cell Membrane

Phospholipid bilayer that controls what enters and leaves the cell. Contains proteins for transport and other functions.

Nucleoid Region

Contains the main circular chromosome (DNA). Not enclosed by a membrane - this is the key difference from eukaryotes!

Plasmids

Small circular DNA molecules separate from the main chromosome. Often carry genes for antibiotic resistance.

Ribosomes

Smaller than eukaryotic ribosomes (70S vs 80S). Site of protein synthesis. Free in cytoplasm.

Cytoplasm

Gel-like substance filling the cell. Contains enzymes and other molecules needed for metabolism.

Binary Fission

Prokaryotes reproduce asexually through a process called binary fission. This is a simple form of cell division where one cell divides into two identical daughter cells.

Stages of Binary Fission
  1. DNA Replication: The circular chromosome is copied
  2. Cell Elongation: The cell grows longer
  3. Chromosome Separation: Copies move to opposite ends
  4. Cytokinesis: Cell membrane pinches in, creating two cells

Under ideal conditions, some bacteria can divide every 20 minutes! This rapid reproduction is why bacterial infections can spread so quickly.

Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic Cells

Feature Prokaryotic Eukaryotic
Nucleus No membrane-bound nucleus (nucleoid) True nucleus with membrane
DNA structure Circular chromosome Linear chromosomes
Organelles No membrane-bound organelles Many membrane-bound organelles
Ribosome size 70S 80S
Cell size 1-10 μm 10-100 μm
Examples Bacteria, Archaea Animals, Plants, Fungi, Protists

Key Definitions:

  • Prokaryote: Organism whose cells lack a membrane-bound nucleus and organelles
  • Nucleoid: Region in prokaryotic cell where DNA is located (not membrane-bound)
  • Plasmid: Small circular DNA molecule found in bacteria, separate from main chromosome
  • Binary Fission: Asexual reproduction method where one cell divides into two identical cells

Assessment Questions

1. Where is the DNA located in a prokaryotic cell?

2. Which structure is found in prokaryotes but NOT in eukaryotes?

Answer Key

Multiple Choice:

  1. B - DNA is in the nucleoid region (not membrane-bound)
  2. A - Plasmids are unique to prokaryotes
