Think About This...

If cells are so important, why aren't we just one giant cell? Why are we made of trillions of tiny cells instead? The answer lies in the mathematics of surface area and volume - and it might surprise you!

What is a Cell?

A cell is the smallest unit of life that can carry out all life processes. Whether it's a bacterium swimming in a pond or a neuron firing in your brain, every living thing is made of cells. Some organisms are just one cell (unicellular), while others - like you - are made of trillions of cells working together (multicellular).

Cell

The basic structural and functional unit of all living organisms. The smallest unit of life that can exist independently.

Cell Theory

Cell theory is one of the fundamental principles of biology. It states three key principles about the nature of cells:

Principle 1

All living organisms are composed of one or more cells.

Principle 2

The cell is the basic unit of structure and organisation in organisms.

Principle 3

All cells come from pre-existing cells through cell division.

Bonus Fact

Energy flows occur within cells, and cells contain hereditary information (DNA).

As a cell grows, its volume increases faster than its surface area. This creates a problem: the cell needs more resources (oxygen, nutrients) but has relatively less surface area to absorb them through.

Surface Area to Volume Ratio (SA:V) is the key concept here. Smaller cells have a higher SA:V ratio, meaning they can exchange materials with their environment more efficiently. When cells get too large, diffusion becomes too slow to meet their needs.

Worked Example: Calculating SA:V Ratio
1

Problem: Calculate the SA:V ratio for a cube with side length 2 cm.

2

Surface Area:

SA = 6 × side² = 6 × 2² = 6 × 4 = 24 cm²
3

Volume:

V = side³ = 2³ = 8 cm³
4

SA:V Ratio:

SA:V = 24 ÷ 8 = 3:1 or simply 3
✓ Answer: The SA:V ratio is 3:1 (or 3)

Key Definitions

  • Cell: The basic structural and functional unit of life
  • Cell Theory: All living things are made of cells; cells are the basic unit; cells come from cells
  • SA:V Ratio: Surface area to volume ratio - determines cell efficiency
  • Unicellular: Organism made of one cell
  • Multicellular: Organism made of many cells with specialized functions

Key Points

  • Cells are small because of the SA:V ratio constraint
  • Smaller cells have higher SA:V ratios (more efficient)
  • Multicellularity allows for cell specialization and larger size
