Think About This...

Your body contains approximately 37 trillion cells - that's more than 4,000 times the number of people on Earth! Yet every single one started from just two cells. How do cells know what to become and what to do?

What is a Cell?

A cell is the smallest unit of life that can carry out all the processes necessary for life. Every living thing is made of cells - from the simplest bacteria to complex multicellular organisms like humans and whales.

Cell: The basic structural, functional, and biological unit of all known living organisms. A cell is the smallest unit of life that can replicate independently.

Cells were first discovered in 1665 by Robert Hooke when he looked at a thin slice of cork through a microscope. He saw tiny compartments that reminded him of monastery cells, hence the name "cells."

Cell Theory

Cell theory is one of the fundamental principles of biology. It consists of three main principles:

1

All living organisms are composed of one or more cells

2

The cell is the basic unit of structure and organisation in organisms

3

All cells come from pre-existing cells (cell division)

What Do All Cells Have?

All Cells Have:

  • Cell membrane (plasma membrane)
  • Cytoplasm
  • DNA (genetic material)
  • Ribosomes (for protein synthesis)

All Cells Can:

  • Obtain and use energy
  • Respond to their environment
  • Grow and develop
  • Reproduce (via cell division)
Deep Dive: The Math Behind Cell Size

As a cell grows larger, its volume increases faster than its surface area. This is because:

  • Surface area increases by the square of the radius (4πr²)
  • Volume increases by the cube of the radius (4/3 πr³)

This means larger cells have proportionally less surface area relative to their volume, making it harder to transport materials across the membrane efficiently.

Worked Example: Calculating SA:V Ratio

Problem: Calculate the surface area to volume ratio of a cube-shaped cell with sides of 2μm.

Solution:

Surface Area = 6 × side² = 6 × (2)² = 6 × 4 = 24 μm²

Volume = side³ = (2)³ = 8 μm³

SA:V Ratio = 24:8 = 3:1

The SA:V ratio is 3:1, meaning the surface area is 3 times the volume.

Copy Into Your Books

Key Definitions:

  • Cell: The basic unit of life; the smallest unit that can carry out all life processes
  • Cell Theory: States that all living things are made of cells, cells are the basic unit of structure, and cells come from pre-existing cells
  • SA:V Ratio: The ratio of surface area to volume - as cells get larger, this ratio decreases, limiting cell size

Key Points:

  • Cells are limited in size by their surface area to volume ratio
  • Prokaryotic cells lack a nucleus; eukaryotic cells have a nucleus
  • All cells have a cell membrane, cytoplasm, DNA, and ribosomes
